## **Part 2: Deep Indian Market Secrets**

### **Secret 1: The Portfolio-Over-Pedigree Approach**
- **Create a "wow" project:** Build something that solves a real Indian problem (traffic, education gaps, local business digitization)
- **Get featured on Indian tech platforms:** Submit to Hashnode (Indian community), Dev.to, share on LinkedIn with relevant hashtags (#IndiaTech #IndianDevelopers)
- **Contribute to Indian open source:** Look for "Digital India" projects, government GitHub repos

### **Secret 2: The Networking Shortcut**
- **LinkedIn Optimization:** 
  - Headline: "Skill-First Developer | [Your Top 3 Skills] | Building [Your Niche]"
  - Connect with Indian tech recruiters with a personalized note: "I admire [Company]'s work in [specific area]. As a developer skilled in [skills], I'd love to connect."
- **Join local tech communities:** 
  - React India, Python India, Hasura communities
  - Attend meetups (even virtual) and **speak up** - ask insightful questions
- **Cold outreach to Indian startup founders:**
  Template: "Hi [Name], I saw [Company] is working on [specific problem]. I built [relevant mini-project] addressing something similar. Would love your thoughts!"

### **Secret 3: The "Prove It First" Strategy**
- **Freelance for Indian clients first:** Upwork, Toptal, even local businesses
- **Build for Indian users:** Create apps with Hindi/regional language support
- **Solve local problems:** Apps for ration shop management, local vendor digitization, regional content platforms

### **Secret 4: Target the Right Companies**
**Priority Order:**
1. **Early-stage Indian startups** (Series A/B) - They care about output, not degrees
2. **Product companies with skill-based hiring** (Zoho, Postman, Freshworks)
3. **Remote-first companies** (global companies hiring in India)
4. **Agencies with project-based work**

**Avoid initially:** Large Indian IT services companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) - they're degree-obsessed
