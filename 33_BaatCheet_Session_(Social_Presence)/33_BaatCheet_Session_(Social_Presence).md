### üîç How to Find and Connect with the Right People
Mastering search is crucial for finding the right contacts beyond your immediate network.

*   **Use Advanced LinkedIn Search**: Don't just type a job title. Start with a blank search (press space + Enter) to access **"All Filters"**. Combine filters like:
    *   **Current Company**: (e.g., "Zeta" or "Razorpay")
    *   **Keywords**: (e.g., "MERN stack" OR "system design")
    *   **Connections of**: (Find 2nd-degree connections you can ask for an introduction)
    *   To find people **outside a specific area** (like major, saturated hubs), you can try using the **`NOT` operator** in the keyword field (e.g., `NOT "Bangalore"`), though results may vary.
*   **Search with a Purpose**: Look for "Senior Software Engineer," "Tech Lead," or "Engineering Manager" at your target companies. These people often influence hiring and can refer you.
*   **The Connection Request**: **Always personalize it**. Mention a specific detail from their profile, a post they shared, or a project their company is working on. Example: *"Hi [Name], I recently built a RAG-based Q&A system and noticed your work on ML at [Company]. I'd be grateful to connect and learn from your insights."*

### üí° Pro Tips for Applying and Getting Noticed
*   **Go Beyond "Easy Apply"**: While Easy Apply is convenient, high-volume jobs get flooded. If you find a job posted by a company employee or hiring manager, apply on the company site *and* send them a polite, concise LinkedIn message mentioning your application.
*   **Build in Public**: This is your most powerful tool. Regularly post small updates:
    *   **On LinkedIn/X**: "Just optimized my API endpoint response time by 200ms. Here's a code snippet showing the bottleneck and fix..."
    *   **On Instagram**: A 15-second reel showing a cool UI animation you built.
*   **Target the Right Companies**: Focus on product-based startups and tech-first companies (like many hiring on platforms such as **X Careers**), as they often prioritize skills and portfolios over formal degrees.
